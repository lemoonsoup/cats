Early Life

  • Although a group of kittens are more commonly called a litter, they can also be known as a “kindle” of kittens.
  • Kittens are born with blue eyes.
  • Kittens are born blind and deaf.
  • While cats usually have an average of four kittens in each litter, this can range from one to 12 kittens.
  • Short hair kittens open their eyes sooner then long hair ones

    • Personality

  • As part of a study, researchers have defined five main personality traits of cats ('Feline Five'), each of which summarises a large number of related traits. The research team grouped them under the terms neuroticism, extraversion, dominance, impulsivity and tolerance.
  • A “slow blink” is your cat's way of saying they love you.
  • Kittens learn from a young age to purr when they are happy and relaxed.
  • Cats are very independent and need much less attention.

    • Features

  • Cats have a set of whiskers on the back of their front legs.
  • Cats are nearsighted, but their peripheral vision and night vision are much better than that of humans.
  • Cats also have excellent hearing and a powerful sense of smell.
  • Some cats are ambidextrous, but 40 percent are either left- or right-pawed.
  • There are 6 varieties of fur patterns that cats can have.

    • Population

  • There are over 500 million domestic cats in the world.
  • Cats and humans have been associated for nearly 10000 years.
  • There are thought to be between 40-70 different cat breeds in the world.
  • There are over 500 million pet cats.
  • One of the largest domestic cat breeds is a Maine Coon.
  • The oldest recorded living cat lived up to 38 years old.

    • Facts

  • A house cat’s genome is 95.6 percent tiger.
  • Cats are believed to be the only mammals who don’t taste sweetness.
  • A group of cats is called a clowder, a male cat is called a tom, a female cat is a molly or queen while young cats are called kittens.
  • Despite popular belief, many cats are actually lactose intolerant.
  • Cats can jump up to six times their length.
  • Cats use their long tails to balance themselves when they’re jumping or walking along narrow ledges.
  • On average cats live for around 12 to 15 years.
